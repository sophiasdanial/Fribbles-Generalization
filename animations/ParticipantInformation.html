Research Purpose

You are invited to participate in a research study. Read the information below and ask questions about anything you donâ€™t understand before deciding if you wish to take part. If you any questions, please email e0771164@nus.edu.sg

This online study is interested in exploring the factors influencing how people identify differences and similarities between items. We are particularly interested in how the way people process visually and how giving examples will affect their judgement.

Who can participate in the research? What is the expected duration of my participation?

Participants should be 21 years old and above.

You should have no significant visual impairments such as colour blindness. It is also necessary to speak or understand English. You must complete this study on a computer.

The study is expected to take up to 15 minutes to complete, and you should finish it within one session. This study will be conducted until August 2023. We aim to recruit 520 participants.

////////////////////////////

What will I do if I take part in this research study?

This study takes place entirely online and will require you to interact with objects on your screen. There will be three phases.

In Phase I, you will undergo a brief training procedure. There will be shapes on the screen that you must identify using the according keyboard board press. In Phase II, you will perform a judgement task, where you will be asked to rate the likelihood of an item belonging to a specific category based on examples you have been given. Lastly, Phase III is a timed reaction task similar to Phase I, where you will have to identify letters as quickly as possible.

What will happen to my data?

This study will not collect any identifying personal data. As such, you will be issued a randomly generated ID number should you need to contact the PI.

Please be assured that all data will be password protected and kept according to NUS Data Management policies. Following the conclusion of this study, the data set will be published under NUS ScholarBank Depository as an open access dataset. This will allow other researchers to analyse and examine the data.

What are the possible discomforts and risks for participants?

Due to the long duration of 15 minutes, there is a possibility of screen fatigue. Do note that between the phases of the study, you will be able to take breaks so long as you do not close the browser window. .

By giving your consent, you will not waive any of your legal rights or release the parties involved in this study from liability for negligence.

Will there be reimbursement for participation?

If you are participating via Amazon Turk, you will be awarded SGD0.78 (or USD0.55) following the conclusion of the study.

What are the possible benefits to me and to others?

There is no direct benefit to you by participating in this research study. The knowledge gained may benefit the public in the future. The study hopes that results gained from this study can go towards furthering knowledge of how and why individuals impacted with impaired perceptual processing, such as autism and anxiety, have difficulty distinguishing between dangerous and non-dangerous stimuli.

Can I refuse to participate in this research?

Yes, you can. Your decision to participate in this research study is voluntary and completely up to you. You can also withdraw from the research at any time without giving any reasons. Just close the study window and all your experimental data will be discarded.

Should you decide to withdraw from the study after submission, please email the PI with your ID number. This will enable us to track and delete your data. Refusal to participate or withdrawal from participation will not result in any consequences whatsoever.


For Amazon Turk participants: Please note that you will not be paid should you decide to withdraw halfway.
